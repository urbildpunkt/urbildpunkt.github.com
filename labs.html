Wearable Seizure Monitor

Physical Prototyping:
Arduino + Processing (Javascript)

Watching a friend develop epilepsy, I saw first-hand the kind of challenges the unpredictability of seizures can cause. Being alone, especially in public, suddenly proves to be a huge safety risk and makes the once mundane routines of everyday life a struggle. Seeing that episodes can go unnoticed even with other family members present in the house is what prompted me to work on this project. Using a homemade arduino programmed with a gps and accelerometer sensor, I prototyped a discrete, wearable device that could detect the motions of a grand mal seizure and call out for help at a time when the wearer himself could not, hopefully bringing back a sense of security and independence. My long-term goal for this project would be to develop a low cost watch that could connect through bluetooth to an app on the wearers phone, which could also provide insights from the data it would collect in order to gain more control and understanding over one’s own seizures.



Digital Wind Chime

Physical Prototyping:
Arduino + Processing (Javascript)

View Project

This was mostly just an art project, the idea of which was to create a physical wind chime that reacted instead to “digital wind.” A lot of the focus was put on the aesthetic, with which I wanted to create an “appliance-like” device that would feel at home on a desk in the same way that a nice clock might, so I created an elegant wood housing for the wind chime that was also able to hide the arduino board in its base. The arduino was programmed to power a vibration motor in reaction to the realtime twitter activity of any chosen search phrase or hastag. For example, a company could actually listen to the flow of their twitter traffic as if it were the breeze blowing a wind chime.



USB Drive Social Network

Physical Prototyping:
Bootable Linux + Buddypress

A physically distributed peer-to-peer social network in which the users themselves actually become the nodes. The users must physically pass the data back and forth in the same way that their data would be passed between the different servers in the network. In limiting their interactions with it to the times in which they have it in their possession, it subverts the traditional norms of a social network such as ubiquity and immediacy in a way that in turn forces them to question the true nature of what it means to be social. While they can’t have the typically instantaneous social interaction on the web, they are also forced into a physical interaction outside of it and so they have to consider which of the two is in fact more social.



Geoquest: Locative MMORPG

Android & Mobile Web App Development

View Project

My goal for this project was to create a locative massively multiplayer online augmented reality role playing game that allowed for user-generated missions. It would provide the platform for a game that the players could then take in any direction. I used Geoloqi’s API to create a location-based event triggering system and added some scavenger-hunt style missions to the map. I also added an IRC-style chat room based on the player’s location, only allowing for communication with players within a certain proximity.



Conversations with the Web,
a Twitter Visualization

Web Design, Javascript

View Project

Being assigned a webdesign prompt no more specific than a “conversation,” I decided to create a twitter visualization that allowed the user to ask general questions of what people all around the world are currently feeling, thinking, hating, etc in that exact moment. It pulls the realtime activity of those phrases so that even while the user doesn’t get to ask any questions to the responders directly, the actual responses are in fact immediate in the same way that they would be in a direct conversation.



Internet as the Public Sphere

Web Design, Javascript

View Project

For my senior project, and as part of my study of hacktivism (electronic civil disobedience) under my professor Ricardo Dominguez of Electronic Disturbance Theatre, I chose to create a Mozilla Popcorn-based “hypervideo” exploring the relationship between social media and politics through the events of the Egyptian revolution that began on the 25 of January, 2011. It was a view of the revolution as it was seen through the many different outlets of social media, including everything from personal tweets and status updates to official public news broadcasts from all over the world. I was asked to present the final product at the 2012 Best of ICAM presentation.
